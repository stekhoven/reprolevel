# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open, thoughtful, and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in this project and its community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We also want this project to support good-faith disagreement. This repository exists to discuss and improve a framework, so critique is welcome when it is offered respectfully, specifically, and with the aim of improving the work.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- using welcoming and inclusive language;
- being respectful of differing viewpoints, backgrounds, and experiences;
- giving constructive, specific, and actionable feedback;
- accepting constructive criticism gracefully;
- focusing on what is best for the project and its community;
- showing empathy toward other community members.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- deliberately dismissive, contemptuous, or humiliating conduct;
- publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without explicit permission;
- other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, or to temporarily or permanently restrict participation for behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and also applies when an individual is representing the project or its community in public spaces. Examples include using an official project email address, posting from an official project account, or acting as a representative of the project at an event or in a public discussion.

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project maintainer:

Daniel J. Stekhoven  
stekhoven@nexus.ethz.ch

Reports will be reviewed and handled as promptly and fairly as possible. The project will make a good-faith effort to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter, while also handling the matter appropriately.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate in response to a Code of Conduct violation, including:

- requesting a change in behavior;
- moderating or removing content;
- closing discussions or issues;
- rejecting contributions;
- temporarily restricting participation;
- permanently banning a participant from project spaces.

Maintainers are expected to apply this Code of Conduct in good faith, consistently, and with attention to context.
